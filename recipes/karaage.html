Fried Chicken Karaage Wrap

Watch out Chick fil-A a new player is in town.

What can I say, it is a deadly combination. Just imaging biting into some crispy fried chicken and following that up with a nice crunch of a lettuce leaf.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken thigh meat, bite size pieces
  • 1/2 grated yellow onion
  • 1/2 grated fuji apple
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp chili pepper
  • 1 tsp chicken bouillon
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 1/2 tsp chili paste
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • Dash of fish sauce
  • Squeeze of lemon
  • Potato starch
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Lettuce

Steps

  1. Start by marinating the chicken thigh pieces. Add the chicken, onion, apple, sake, soy sauce, chili pepper, bouillon, and salt and pepper to a bag or a bowl. Massage the chicken a good 3-4 minutes, and set aside in the fridge for at least thirty minutes to take on some of that flavor.
  2. Meanwhile, make the dipping sauce. Add the chili paste, ketchup, fish sauce, and lemon juice to a bowl, and stir. Set aside till ready to use.
  3. Bring vegetable oil to 350 F. Remove the karaage from the marinade and coat liberally in potato starch. I find this easiest to do by plopping the individual pieces of chicken into a pile of starch, and then popping them directly in the oil. Of course, you can just toss the chicken in a bag of potato starch, but remember to remove as much of the marinade as possible to avoid over-clumping. Fry in batches of 4-5 to maintain oil temperature, about 3 minutes per side. Occasionally lift out of the oil to mimic the double-frying technique. Allow to drain/cool on a paper towel.
  4. Serve with lettuce leaves of your choice. Guests can take a few pieces of karaage, wrap them in the leaves, and dip liberally in the chili dipping sauce. Enjoy!
